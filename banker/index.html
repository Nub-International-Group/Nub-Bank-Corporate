Frequently Asked Questions

What can I actually do in Nub Bank?

There is an absolute litany of things possible through Nub Banks integrated system or by making peer-to-peer transactions, to help get your creative juices flowing we've listed a few of our favourite below:

  • Bribery
  • Design, Consulting or other Service Work
  • Press Appearances/Article Writing
  • Speeches
  • Cash for Honours/Peerages Scandals
  • Donations
  • Money Laundering
  • Stocks and Shares Trading (Player-Run Markets)
  • Company Formation
  • Cash for Votes Scandals
  • Betting
  • Luxury Purchases
  • Asset Investments
  • Gifts
  • Awards/Prizes
  • Advertisement Fees
  • Dividends
  • Player-run gambling/casinos

Wait, you charge tax?

Sure do, we charge tax based on the rates of Income Tax in the most recent passed budget on MHOC. We do not simulated National Insurance or Capital Gains. Due to the volume and size of the transactions that happen within our economy, we've have to implement a transaction fee *gasp*. You can see a list of them above in the accounts summary. These can change, but everyone will be notified in advance.

Tell me about assets, how do they work?

Players can buy, sell and build property to make money and accumulate assets. Assets are easily traded between players in any method they choose (selling, gifting, company takeover, auctions etc). Some assets will generate an income, some will be purely value-based.

The value of an asset is determined by the market, when initially created a value will be provided by Nub Bank, every time an asset switches hands the value will be updated as players must input the buying price for the asset. Historic values will always tag with the asset, so if an asset has sold for £1 you'll still be able to see it was originally worth £150,000. If you want an asset to be valued before you sell it (to make sure it's still a fair value), speak to the bank staff.

Who creates assets then?

Certain companies may construct assets for themselves or be contracted to create a particular asset for another player, all profits from the sale of the asset will go to them instead of Nub Bank.

There will be some risk and some reward when it comes to property, regular income won't be shown to anyone but the owner and you will be taxed (classifications will be given to give you an idea) and eventually we will move to simulate a whole asset market, with technology infrastructure letting us drop house prices in Leeds, while boosting them in Glasgow.

Famous assets will be available but some will be restricted to certain accounts (The governing party will get 10 Downing Street for example).

So whats this betting lark?

Betting is the lifeblood of the British high street. Whether it be on sports, horse racing, politics or major events the British population love a good wager. Nub Bank is happy to offer its customers an exclusive betting system that is built into the website, for ease of use.

Bets will be announced in our Discord and players will be able to bet up to £1,000,000.00 (one million) on any bet, from any of their eligible accounts. Players may make multiple bets or spread bets and can bet as many times as they like.

Will businesses need to file accounts?

Businesses will need to file accounts at a yet to be determined interval, these accounts will help the bank with business valuation and general housekeeping. Businesses that do not submit in time will be sanctioned. If you need an accountant, I'm sure there'll be a business for that!

How do businesses get regular income and pay staff?

Some businesses will have difficulty staying afloat as the nature of their work is unpredictable (see Press Work) Nub Bank wants to support these businesses. Once your business is open you'll receive an Articles of Association asset, which guarantees you a small minimum income (randomly generated based on your sector). If staff want to draw a wage from a business, they will need to do so from that business itself, Nub Bank will no longer provide these wages.

What is your Enforcement Policy?

Nub Bank will take steps to ensure the integrity of it's banking system. While consumers have the ability to break laws and act dishonestly/deceptively, it does not mean the bank will allow it.

  • Customers who default on Peer-to-Peer or Corporate Loans can (at the request of the lender) be made insolvent.
  • Customers who commit wage fraud will be sanctioned by the bank.
  • Customers who attempt to hide or launder money or the proceeds of crime will be sanctioned by the bank.
  • The bank reserves the right to sanction users and will provide justification for enforcement action where requested. Customers may appeal.
